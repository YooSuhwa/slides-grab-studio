# Table

Structured data in rows and columns. Best for multi-criteria comparison.

## Principles

**Tables are for comparison.** When you need to compare several items on the same criteria side by side, tables are the most effective format.

**Header row must be distinct.** Use bold weight, background color, or a divider line to separate headers from data rows.

**Keep cell content short.** Keywords or numbers. If sentences creep in, it's a document, not a slide table.

**Minimize rows and columns.** On a slide, 5 rows × 4 columns is about the readable limit. Beyond that, split into 2 slides or provide as a handout.

## Avoid

- Tables with 6+ rows (becomes a spreadsheet)
- Long text in cells
- Heavy borders on every cell (row separators are enough)
- Font size shrinking to fit (if it's unreadable, it's useless)
