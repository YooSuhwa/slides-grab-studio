# Content Slide

The workhorse. Presents one topic with a title and supporting text.

## Principles

**Scannable in 3 seconds.** The audience glances at the slide while listening. The title alone should convey the key point; body text is supplementary.

**One slide, one topic.** If you have two topics, use two slides. Two relaxed slides beat one cramped slide.

**Clear gap between title and body.** Both size and weight should differ noticeably — this gap creates hierarchy. Body text should feel much lighter and smaller than the title.

## Structures

- Title + body paragraph (simplest, 2-4 lines)
- Title + bullet list (3-5 points, 1-2 lines each)
- Title + subtitle + body (subtitle adds context)

If content exceeds 6 points, switch to two-columns or split into 2 slides.

## Adapt

- Little content (1-2 sentences): enlarge the title, let whitespace breathe.
- Too much content: compress into bullets or split into 2 slides.
- Single strong statement: highlight type may be more appropriate.

## Avoid

- Filling more than 60% of the slide with text
- Nested bullets (no sub-bullets)
- Center-aligned body text (left-align reads better)
- Images on a content slide (use split-layout or image-text instead)
