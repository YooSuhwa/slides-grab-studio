# Cover Slide

The first slide. Sets the topic and visual tone for the entire deck.

## Principles

**Whitespace is the design.** A cover says very little. Title and subtitle — that's it. The empty space itself communicates confidence.

**Title is the largest, heaviest element on the slide.** Use the biggest size and boldest weight the pack offers. This sets the baseline for the whole deck.

**Subtitle is quiet.** One line that supports the title. It must feel clearly subordinate — much lighter, much smaller.

## Content

- Title + subtitle only. Nothing else.
- If a label/tag is needed (e.g., "Q4 2025"), place it small above the title.
- No images, lists, or paragraphs.
- If the title wraps to 3+ lines, it's too long — shorten it.

## Adapt

- Short title (1-3 words): go bigger, more dramatic whitespace.
- Long title (8+ words): step down one size, allow 2 lines.
- Dark-background pack: invert text colors.
- Choose left-aligned or centered based on pack mood.

## Avoid

- More than 3 elements (title + subtitle + optional label is the max)
- Body-sized title (cover must feel grand)
- Decorations not defined in the pack's signature
