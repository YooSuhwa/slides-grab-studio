# Timeline

Shows chronological progression — milestones, roadmaps, process steps.

## Principles

**Time should feel like it flows.** The most natural direction is left-to-right (horizontal). People intuit time moving from left to right. But vertical works too when each step needs more detail.

**Milestones are evenly spaced.** Even if real time gaps differ, distribute equally on the slide. Uneven spacing creates false emphasis.

**Nodes and connecting lines are supporting actors.** The content (dates, titles, descriptions) is the star. Nodes are small position markers, not decorative features. Connecting lines guide the eye without drawing attention.

**Distinguish the current/highlighted step.** If one milestone is special (current phase, next goal), give it subtle visual emphasis — different from the rest but not screaming.

## Adapt

- 2-3 milestones: spacious, descriptions can be longer.
- 4-5: standard, keep descriptions to 1-2 lines.
- 6+: trim to keywords, split into 2 slides, or try vertical layout.
- Detailed 3-4 items: vertical layout may work better.

## Avoid

- Different colors per milestone (highlight one, unify the rest)
- Diagonal or curved connecting lines (straight is clean)
- Oversized nodes (content is the star, not the dots)
- Forcing long descriptions into a single row
